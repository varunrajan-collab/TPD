Step 5: Interpretation

This pattern often reflects a moment where awareness of the classroom was active, and the balance between following a plan and responding to immediate signals was in play.

Such patterns typically show that noticing student cues was happening, with varying levels of responsive adjustment in the moment.

Step 6: Connection to Impact

When teachers notice and adjust in these moments, it typically allows students to feel more seen and can prevent early confusion from growing.

This often leads to increased student engagement and more effective differentiation of instruction in real-time.
